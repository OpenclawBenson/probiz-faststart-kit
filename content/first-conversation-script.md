# First Conversation Script
## How to Recruit Your First (or Next) Contractor in 15 Minutes

---

## The Mindset

You're not "selling" ProBiz.

You're having a conversation with someone who has a problem (need for leads) and showing them a solution.

That's it. No pressure. No persuasion. Just help.

---

## The 3-Question Framework

### Question 1: The Problem Reveal
**"How do you typically get new customers right now?"**

**What you're listening for:**
- Word of mouth (unpredictable)
- Google Ads (expensive)
- Facebook/Instagram (time-consuming)
- HomeAdvisor/Angi (paying for leads, low quality)
- Nothing consistent (the struggle)

**Why this works:** Everyone has a pain point around lead generation. You're just revealing it.

---

### Question 2: The Cost of Inaction
**"What's that costing you — either in money, time, or missed opportunities?"**

**What you're listening for:**
- "I'm spending $500/month on ads"
- "I waste 2 hours a day on social media"
- "Slow season is brutal"
- "I never know where the next job is coming from"

**Why this works:** They articulate the pain in their own words. Now they FEEL it.

---

### Question 3: The Solution Transition
**"What if you had a steady stream of qualified leads coming from local business owners who already trust you?"**

**Pause. Let them respond.**

They'll say something like:
- "That sounds amazing"
- "How does that work?"
- "I'd love that"

**Now you have permission to share ProBiz.**

---

## The ProBiz Introduction (30 seconds)

**"I'm part of a local referral network called ProBiz.Cards. We connect contractors with customers through digital business cards and local business partnerships.

Here's how it works: Local business owners — realtors, property managers, insurance agents — they refer their customers to contractors in our network. The contractors get qualified leads. The business owners get a referral bonus. Everyone wins.

I'm building my own mini-directory of contractors I can refer to these business owners. That's why I wanted to talk to you."

---

## Handling Common Responses

### "How much does it cost?"
**"There's no cost to be listed in my directory. When you get a lead through the network, there's a small referral fee — but you only pay when you get business. Most contractors find it's way cheaper than advertising."

### "How many leads can I expect?"
**"That depends on your trade and the area. Some contractors get 2-3 leads a week. Others get 2-3 a month. The key is you're getting warm referrals from trusted sources — not cold leads from the internet."

### "What's the catch?"
**"No catch. I'm building my network. You're getting leads. The customer gets a reliable contractor. It's a three-way win. The only thing I ask is that you provide good service to anyone I refer — because my reputation is on the line too."

### "I'm pretty busy already"
**"That's actually a good problem to have. Most contractors in the network start part-time — they just take the leads they can handle. As you grow, the lead flow grows with you."

### "Let me think about it"
**"Of course. Here's what I'll do — I'll send you a link to my ProBiz directory so you can see how it works. Take a look, and if it makes sense, we can get you set up. Fair?"

*(Get their email/phone and follow up within 24 hours)*

---

## The Close (Soft)

**"Based on what you told me, it sounds like this could solve your [specific pain point they mentioned]. Want me to send you the info so you can check it out?"

**If yes:**
- Get their best email
- Send the ProBiz signup link immediately
- Follow up in 24-48 hours

**If maybe:**
- "No pressure. Can I check back with you next week?"
- Set a reminder and follow up

**If no:**
- "No problem at all. If things change or you know another contractor who might be interested, feel free to pass my info along."
- Stay friendly. They might refer someone else.

## Pro Tips

1. **Listen more than you talk.** The goal is to understand their pain, not pitch ProBiz.

2. **Take notes.** Write down their specific pain points and use their words in the close.

3. **Don't oversell.** If they're not interested, move on. There are plenty of contractors who need leads.

4. **Follow up.** 80% of sales happen after the 5th follow-up. Most people give up after 1.

5. **Track everything.** Use the Contractor Tracker Spreadsheet to know where every prospect stands.

That's valuable. Act like it.
